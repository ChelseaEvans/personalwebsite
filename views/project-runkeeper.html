OVERVIEW

THE PROBLEM

Business Overview: Track your pace, measure workout distance, chart weight loss, crush training goals and more with LifeHacker’s 2012 Best Running App! RunKeeper is the simplest way to improve fitness, whether you’re just deciding to get off the couch for a 5k, biking every day, or deep into marathon training.

Lots of RunKeeper users who have an exercise routine report weather playing a large role in their fitness behavior. One way weather influences them is in scheduling their outdoor exercise (and the rest of their life) around favorable weather conditions. Focus on solving the problem of "when should I workout" based on the weather.

2.5 weeks, group project with Kara and Rena.

TESTING

Following multiple rounds of testing, I received the following feedback from the user: She doesn’t want to have to name the routes herself (they should auto name), wants to be able to save routes, wants to be able to reuse routes, some of the labels are vague and/or confusing, she wants to be able to access information about a route that she has used (i.e. average travel time, be able to see the map, etc.), wants the ability to ‘navigate’ if it’s a route she doesn’t know as well.

PROTOTYPES & ITERATIONS

Based on user feedback I reiterated several times. This is where I really fleshed out the functionality and details:

  • Auto-naming convention for routes
  • Route is auto-saved once inputs have been entered
  • A user can reuse a route and is brought back to the edit screen in case they want to modify something about the route.
  • The alert notification is defaulted to 5 minutes; what does this look like?
  • The REPEAT and USED ROUTES screens will display certain information relevant to the user.
  • Careful consideration of word use, placement of items, buttons, etc.
  • What’s clickable? How do I let the user know?
  • Final check: Am I meeting all of the user’s requirements?

LESSONS LEARNED

  • Don’t get hung up on specifics too early and keep in mind what the app is actually intended to do; what are the most important user requirements?
  • Reduce overhead for the user.
  • Reduce the number of screens where the user needs to enter information for faster loading.
  • Carefully consider language used to concisely convey the right message and instructions to the user.
  • Keep an open mind about various uses and don’t make narrow assumptions (I am not the user!!): I was stuck on one use-case of traveling from home to work, assuming that Hitomi already knew where to go and I did not consider she would need a map in order to navigate her way.
